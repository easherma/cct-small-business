Despite their vision, tenacity and commitment, entrepreneurs of color face significant barriers to success.

There are more than 135 business support organizations positioned throughout the city, offering a wide range of services for entrepreneurs, and yet the barriers for entrepreneurs of color persist.

What is lacking is coordination among these organizations to provide targeted support to address the issues faced specifically by entrepreneurs of color.

Our theory is that if business servicing organizations are connected to one another and given the tools and technical assistance to expand partnerships with other providers, they will be able to offer more strategic and impactful services for entrepreneurs of color.

This will build stronger businesses of color throughout the Chicago area that will in turn begin to grow more sustainable wealth for communities of color.

Over the course of the program, we anticipate an increase in:

  • the number of small businesses assisted throughout Chicago.
  • the number of entrepreneurs that start-up viable businesses.
  • the number of quality jobs created and retained within the communities served.
  • revenue for small businesses.
  • investment attracted by both the business servicing organizations and the small businesses served.
  • knowledge of financial management for businesses.
  • the number of entrepreneurs demonstrating fundamental business finance skills and tools for growth.
  • Internal capacity from businesses servicing organizations and strength of their collaborations.
  • access for entrepreneurs of color to a trusted network of local small business professionals and community groups.
